Why farmers use Togro

Fewer gates left open. Fewer dogs near livestock.

Log your livestock

Set which fields have animals and when. Walkers on those paths see it before they go.

Flag a risk in seconds

Bull in a field, a broken fence, a blocked gate, flooding. Pin it and walkers are warned.

Fewer incidents

Heads-ups mean gates stay shut, dogs stay on leads, and walkers keep their distance.

Walkers see it live

Your advisories reach every walker on a route crossing your land, in real time.

Recognised, not charged

Farmers never pay and are never paid cash in v1. You earn credits and public recognition.

Well-managed traffic

Verified, informed walkers, plus venue revenue if you have a farm shop or cafe.

How it works

A few minutes, then it runs itself.

1

Verify as a landowner

Confirm your land once. Bulk options if you onboard through the NFU, CLA or a Young Farmers group.

2

Log livestock and flag risks

Add what is in each field and when, and pin any hazards. Update any time from your phone.

3

Walkers crossing get a live heads-up

Anyone on a route through your land sees your advisories before and during their walk.

4

You are recognised

Earn credits and public acknowledgement on the routes you help keep safe.

How farmers are recognised (not charged)

Farmer participation is what makes the map trustworthy, so we treat it as the supply side, not a sales target.

  • Never pay, never paid cash in v1. Recognition is Togro credits and public acknowledgement on routes.
  • Stewardship recognition. An annual rural-stewardship programme celebrating the farmers who keep access working.
  • Grandfathered upside. Farmers who join now are first in line for revenue share if a paid land-data product launches.
Farmer Ambassador Programme

For the farmers and landowner groups who want to bring others on board and shape how Togro works on the ground.
