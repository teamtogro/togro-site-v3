About Togro

Built for the countryside, by the people who love it.

Togro exists to make the countryside safer, fairer and easier to enjoy: a living map kept honest by the people who walk it and work it. Walkers always come first: the app is free, and contributing is rewarded, never charged. Farmers, venues, councils and brands join to support that network, not to mine it. Walkers stay anonymous, always.

Why we built it

The countryside deserved better than a guess.

Too many walks start with a guess. Is the path flooded? Is there a bull in that field? Has the bridge washed out? People set off not knowing, and the people who do know, the farmers, the councils, the walkers who passed an hour ago, had no simple way to pass it on.

Togro joins them up. Walkers report what they find from the path, and everyone heading that way sees it. Farmers can flag livestock and hazards in seconds. Councils can push official notices and see where people actually go. And local pubs, cafes and farm shops can welcome the walkers coming past their door. One live map, built by the community that uses it.

What we believe

A few things we will not budge on.

Free for walkers, always

The people out on the paths are the heart of Togro. They will never be charged to use it.

Totals only, never names

Venues, councils and brands see real visits in aggregate. We never share who individual walkers are.

Built with the countryside

Farmers, councils and walkers shape Togro. We build with the people who know the land, not over their heads.
