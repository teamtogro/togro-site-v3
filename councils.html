Keep your paths open, your visitors safe.

Togro gives the people walking your rights of way a simple way to report what they find, flooding, blocked paths, broken stiles, fly-tipping, livestock, and gives you a live picture of what is happening on the ground. You can push official notices straight to walkers before they set off, and see where people actually go, with totals only and no names.

Why it helps

A clearer view of your rights of way.

Problems reported as they happen

Walkers flag blocked paths, broken stiles, flooding, fallen trees and fly-tipping from the spot, with a photo and a GPS location.

Tell walkers before they set off

Push closures, diversions and seasonal notices straight to people heading your way, so fewer ignore a sign they never saw.

See where people actually go

Understand the busy spots and the quiet ones so you can spread visitors out and look after the places under most pressure. Totals only, never names.

Fewer incidents on the land

Clear, timely warnings about livestock, lambing and farm work mean fewer conflicts between walkers, dogs and the people who work the land.

Reports you can trust

Every report is from a real person who was actually there, confirmed by GPS and by other walkers, so you are acting on what is real.

Free for the public

The app is free for walkers, so the more people use it, the better your picture of the ground becomes, at no cost to residents.

How it works

Set up once, then it runs itself.

1

We map your area

We bring your rights of way and any current closures or notices onto the map for your patch.

2

Walkers report from the ground

People using the paths flag what they find. You see it come in, confirmed and located, in near real time.

3

You push notices back out

Send official closures, diversions and seasonal warnings to walkers before they reach the problem.

4

You see the bigger picture

Visit totals and pressure points help you plan maintenance, manage access and look after the busy spots. No names, ever.
